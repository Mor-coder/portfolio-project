Do you remember the last time you booked a holiday? Dit it all go as smooth as silk or were there any hiccups?

It could be that little miscommunication with the hotel, or a discrepancy between the room shown online and the reality of it, or the different perception of "short distance" to the local amnesties - which ends up in you purchasing an unexpected, and frankly not even needed, new pair of shoes because the sole of yours is gone... Most of the time - that I know of from personal or through a friend's experience - these hiccups can either be solved there and then or there isn't much anyone can do about it so you just zen out and enjoy the rest of the holiday.

The point is, if I were to build a hotel tomorrow, how would I design the booking process to provide a kick-ass experience? How do the best in the field do?


When you don't know, research - When you do know, research anyway. Chances are great minds already came up with some lovely options you haven't thought about.

For this project, I opted for using the Competitive benchmarking + Usability test combo. With competitive benchmarking the aim was to gain an understanding of the best practices to adopt when designing a hotel booking process. While researching and taking notes, I could already find most of the answers to my questions but still, that is my perception, what do other people think?
