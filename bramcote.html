The Bramcote Old Church Tower's website re-design - behind the scene

The context -

Archaeological sites are like a portal to the past. They allow you to have a blurred yet undeniably real sneak peek into the history of humanity. Nevertheless, despite their role as history representatives, they don't always receive the care and attention they deserve.

The de-consecrated 13th-century Bramcote Old Church Tower and the surrounding graveyard are one of those sites that, thanks to the dedication and passion of the local community still have a story to tell.

The desire to translate in the website the enthusiasm and hard work that the Friends of the Old Church Tower Trust puts daily into this project is what prompted me to offer my help and initiate the research process.

Dig deeper, build empathy -

Comes first, I sat down with the web designer to discuss the what's, the whys and the hows behind the website's structure. Slowly but surely, more information on the website and the project's past, present, and future came to the surface, allowing me to grasp the Trust's mission and identity. We opted for runnig a series of Usability tests to identify how people felt about the website and to unfold the following:

  • We need to add a new page called "History". Where would people naturally look for it?
  • Do people know how to reach us?
  • Do they know the Trust run events and how to participate?

as well as more sensitive questions, like:

  • How do we motivate people to get involved?
  • How can we prompt them to donate without being too pushy?

The findings -

The Usability tests produced significant and insightful data that start shaping the face of the soon-to-be refreshed website. These included:

・The need to show peoples' immediate needs on the homepage. These included directions to the site and information such as, opening hours, parking availability, and contacts.
・Where to add the link to the new 'History' page.
・How to improve the events page to foster participation.
・Perhaps the most tricky one, how to engage with people and prompt them to get involved or donate.
