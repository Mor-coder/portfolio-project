Weather App Project

As my main objective was learning how to code
and integrate API in my project, I didn't literally
follow the UX design process. Instead, I opted for
taking bits and bobs from each of the five steps
and integrate them in the design/coding process.

I created persona based on observation and informal conversations with people. Starting with small talk (literally about the weather!), I collected informations on how this might be affecting their day. From there, the conversation would follow a natural flow revealing in more details how the weather impact their day to day routine, or has had an impact in organising special occasions.

Finally I would ask if they rely on any weather app. Given that in general the weather forecast is perceived as not 100% accurate and temperatures are perceived differently by each individual, it was curious and intriguing understanding how people engage with weather apps, for what purposes, and how much they rely on them.

On top of those observations I noticed that slang phrases, such as "It's raining cats and dogs", are frequently used to describe the weather conditions and, funny enough, may be more effective in delivering the message.

By contrast, temperatures expressed more traditionally in degrees, proved a bit tricky as each person perceives them differently, although he/she/they would still have a general idea of the weather conditions.

NOTES: Although slang phrases might be more effective, they are specific to the geographical location they are used, making them less appropriate for a global audience.

Degrees, on the other hand, are universal numbers largerly understood by a greater majority.

Once moved away from the idea of adding descriptive elements and embracing the more classic numerical one, I worked on a few technical elements:

  • One of the testing session taught me that many people decided to live on the canal waters and the WIND condition is as important as refilling the water/gas tank.
  • To improve the general idea of the weather condition, and hopefully allow people to get a better feeling of the outdoor temperaure, I also added HUMIDITY and a SHORT DESCRIPTION.
  • To re-inforce the button feature, I enabled the pointer option when the cursor moves on "Search".
  • Then, I had some fun playing around with the web page background and the app background. As the app is showing the weather forecats, I liked for the background to resemble the colour of the sky, possibly a clear sky above the clouds (like the one you see when flying up in the sky). On top of that, I wanted it to somehow interact with the main page background, so I tried few options mainly portraying different the sky at different times of the day, like sunsets or sunrise (adding to them a pinch of fantasy).

Eventually, I recalibrated the whole situation and changed persepective slightly. For the main page I opted for a black background, like the universe, with two half circles coloured in different hues of orange on the left representing the sun. For the app background, I animated background with gradient colours going from warm to cold variations in an infinite loop. The idea was to reproduce the daily motion of the earth around the sun, going from morning to evening.
