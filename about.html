Who's that girl?

SPOILER ALERT: This story doesn't have a happy ending...well, actually it doesn't have an end (yet)!. You can rest assured that it is full of trials and errors, certainties and doubts, ups and downs. Perhaps some people will relate to it. Either way this is how it goes...

[For the short version check out my CV]

Some people seem to know exactly what they want to do in life. Well, that person wasn't me. I wanted to do so many things I didn't even know where to start but, above all, I wanted to do something that would make me happy and satisfied of myself, which is insanily hard when you are the most severe judge of yourself!.

My adventure kicked off with a BA in interactive communication at the Interdisciplinary Center Herlziya (ISR), where the door to the fascinating world of human-machine interaction has been opened in front of me.

That was mindblowing and yet, I still felt like I was missing something so I moved to the smoky London looking for fortune. There I enrolled in a Pg.Dip (although we will see later that I only gained a Pg.Cert) the creative, yet structured, world of graphic design. The grid system killed me, I now understand it but back then it killed me so slowly but surely I started navigating towards the unstransparent, eccentric, and utterly fascinating world of street and contemporary art.

"Some people see things for what they are, others for what they mean"


(extract from the book "Zen and the art of motorcycle maintenance")

Since then it has been a roller coster of experiences covering varied roles within the art galleries/events circuit. Despite the wonderful experience, I was missing the connection and challenge of trying to solve problems related to people as individuals and as a whole, with the help of technologies.

Sometimes, you come across a sentence in a book and those few words open up your mind to new perspectives that you just didn't see before.

To understand things for what they mean, as opposed to what they are, it's one of those. For me at least. When applied to the field of art or UX design, for instance, it helps me emphatise with the people I design the website, or app, for. I am not just thinking of creating an interactive, intuitive, and aesthetically pleasing object, I also take into account what the object mean; what are the feelings connected to the need fulfilled by the object. Incorporating the human element to the design process makes everything a little bit deeper, way more complicated, but ultimately more interesting and fun.
