My story

SPOILER ALERT: this is not a traditional success story, on the contrary, is the story of someone who, through trial and error, found her way.

Some people seem to know exactly what they want to do in life, at least career wise. Well, that person wasn't me. Some of my ideal career path included: being a music journalist, a food critic, a dancer, a graphic designer, a gallery director, and, although I never really said it out loud, an artist.

As I had to start from somewhere, I started off by enrolling in a BA in communication at the Interdisciplinary Center Herzlyia where I had a radio program with a friend, got totally absorbed in discourses rotating psychology, tech, and people (aka UX/UI design) and tipped my toes in the wicked world of web design.

Despite all the fun in the sun and the incredible knowledge I gained, I was still a bit lost. From here, the decision to move from the land of milk and honey to the smoky London to gain further knowledge of visual communication (aka graphic design).

All of a sudden, billboards, posters, and flyers never looked so good. Typography and printing techniques was something I was particularly fascinated by, especially letterpress and screenprinting. I liked to get my hands dirty.
