Who's that girl?


[For the short version check out my CV]

Some people seem to know exactly what they want to do in life. Well, that person wasn't me. My background is a roller coster of experiences within the art industry, and the strenuous pursue of a job that would make me feel satisfied with myself.

Nevertheless, it all led me to gain the courage to start anew and take with me valuable lessons. Art thaught me to look at the context in which an artwork has been created to fully appreciate the meaning behind it, and that there isn't a right or wrong answer but interpretations of the same work. This is what I took with me when I switched to UX design and coding. I feel that to create meaningful experiences you got to understand the context, the people, and the issues involved. Coding, really, came along as I felt that using platforms already providing websites' structure was a bit like cheating. I needed to grasp the languages behind websites to provide better, more engaging, and creative experiences.

"Some people see things for what they are, others for what they mean"


(extract from the book "Zen and the art of motorcycle maintenance")

Sometimes, you come across a sentence in a book, or the lyrics of a song, or a scene in a movie that opens up your mind to perspectives that you just didn't see before. The time and form each media takes, provides a different experience, and as unique individuals, each experience speak to us in unique ways.

To understand things for what they mean, as opposed to what they are, it's one of those. For me at least. It gave me a new perspective on almost everything, probably everything. What's a website? Or a pen? Or a friend? They're not just a digital page, or ink in a plastic tube, or flesh and blood. They are also respectively a source of information (and knowledge and power), or a way to express yourself, or a safe place.
