Who's that girl?


SPOILER ALERT: This story doesn't have a happy ending...well, it doesn't have an end (yet)!. Nevertheless, you can rest assured that it is full of trials and errors, certainties and doubts, ups and downs. Perhaps some people will relate to it. Either way this is how it goes...

[For the short version check out my CV]

Some people seem to know exactly what they want to do in life. Well, that person wasn't me. I wanted to do something that would make me happy and satisfied with myself, which is insanily hard when you are a painfully perfectionist virgo aka the most severe judge of yourself!.

My adventure kicked off at the Interdisciplinary Center Herlziya (ISR), where the door to the fascinating world of human-machine relationship has been opened in front of me. That was mindblowing and yet, I still felt like I was missing something so I moved to the smoky London looking for fortune. There I met the creative, yet structured, world of visual communication aka graphic design-ish. I loved it and hated it, if it possible but more so, I was craving something more rebellious, alternative, underground, bohemian, so I immersed myself in the world of fine art...What a marvellous clich√©!(Yes, I can hear you laughing and I am laughing with you!). Nevertheless, I did learn a thing or two and add them to the bucket of lessons I've been carrying with me since 1988.

"Some people see things for what they are, others for what they mean"


(extract from the book "Zen and the art of motorcycle maintenance")

Since then it has been a roller coster of experiences covering varied roles within the art world, going back to study and so on and so forth. And yet, without that I wouldn't be here and you wouldn't be reading the drama of my life (can't help my dramatic Italianess from coming out) so Happy Days!

Sometimes, you come across a sentence in a book and those few words open up your mind to new perspectives that you just didn't see before. That's why reading provides a different experience from listening to music or watching something. It's a matter of time you give yourself to appreciate and makes yours what someone else has suggested to you.

To understand things for what they mean, as opposed to what they are, it's one of those. For me at least. It gave me a new perspective on almost everything, probably everything. What's a website? Or a pen? Or a friend? They're not just a digital page, or ink in a plastic tube, or flesh and blood. They are also sources of information (and knowledge and power), a way to express yourself, a safe place.
