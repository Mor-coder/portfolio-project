Who's that girl?

SPOILER ALERT: This story doesn't have a happy ending...well, actually it doesn't have an end (yet)!. However, you can rest assured that it is full of trials and errors, certainties and doubts, ups and downs. Perhaps some people will relate to it. Either way this is how it goes...

[For the short version check out my CV]

Some people seem to know exactly what they want to do in life. Well, that person wasn't me. I wanted to do so many things I didn't even know where to start but, above all, I wanted to do something that would make me happy and satisfied.

My adventure kicked off with a BA in interactive communication at the Interdisciplinary Center Herlziya (ISR), where the door to the fascinating world of human-machine interaction has been opened in front of me.

That was mindblowing and yet, I still felt like I was missing something so I move to the smoky London looking for fortune. There I approached the creative, yet structured, world of graphic design which led me to what it would be a full immersion into the art world.

Since then it has been a roller coster of experiences covering varied roles within the art galleries/events circuit. Despite the wonderful experience, I was missing the connection and challenge of trying to solve problems related to people as individuals and as a whole, with the help of technologies.

"Some people see things for what they are, others for what they mean"


(extract from the book "Zen and the art of motorcycle maintenance")

Sometimes, you come across a sentence in a book and those few words simply blow up your mind as they open your eyes to new perspectives that you just didn't see before. And yet, they are so true and so common.

Understanding this for what they mean, as opposed to what they are, it's one of those. For me at least. When applied to the field of UX design, for instance, it helped me emphatise with the people I was designing the application or website for. I wasn't only thinking of creating an interactive, intuitive, and aesthetically pleasing object, all of a sudden I was thinking about what that would mean for them. What were the feelings connected to the need the app or website was answering. Everything became a little bit deeper, way more interesting.
