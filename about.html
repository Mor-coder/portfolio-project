Who's that girl?

SPOILER ALERT: This is a story that maybe some people will relate to. It doesn't have an end (yet) but you can rest assured it is full of trials and errors, certainties and doubts, ups and downs...

Check the CV for the short answer...

Some people seem to know exactly what they want to do in life. Well, that person wasn't me. I wanted to do so many things I didn't even know where to start but, above all, I wanted to do something that would make me happy and satisfied.

My academic adventure kicked off with a BA in interactive communication at the Interdisciplinary Center Herlziya (ISR), where the door to the fascinating world of human-machine interaction has been opened in front of me.

That was mindblowing and yet, I still felt like I was missing something so I move to the smoky London looking for furtune. There I approached the creative, yet structured, world of graphic design which led me to what it would be a full immersion into the art galleries circuit.

Since then it has been a roller coster of experiences covering varied roles within the art world. Despite the wonderful experience, I was missing the challenge of trying to solve human problems with the help of technologies, started in my college years.

"Some people see things for what they are, others for what they mean"

This pearl of wisdom from the book "Zen and the art of motorcycle maintenance" blew my mind and gave me a new perspective on how to approach design for people. An app, for instance, can be seen as a technical device providing us with X and Y information and that is pretty much it.

To the same perception, it can be added the emotional factor. What are the needs the app is answering to and what are the feelings and emotions surrounding our needs?

If it is a weather app, for instance, we might be looking at it because we need to know how man layers we need to out on before we leave the nest for the day, or we might be planning a day out (yay!), or a birthday party at the park (which might be moved to the pub if the weather is variable as the British one). All these factors must be taken into account when designing an app as we, human beings, are emotional creatures and driven by feelings other than reason.
